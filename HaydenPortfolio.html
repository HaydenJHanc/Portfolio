Bivariate Analysis of Crop Yield

Dr. Liang, GEO 309 (Introduction to GIS) Spring 2023

For this project, I wanted to go beyond making a simple map and explore using GIS for analysis. I used ArcGIS to overlay the data my family's farm has on yield and moisture in fields during harvest, and overlaid it on publicly accessible factors like elevation and soil to see what factions could be attributed to good/poor yield. This concept will be expanded on in my GEO 560 Independent Study.

Tracking Urban Development in the D.P.R. Korea

Dr. Liang, GEO 419 (Introduction to Remote Sensing) Spring 2023

GEO 419 was all about remote sensing. An interesting aspect of remote sensing is that it allows us to collect data on areas where data would otherwise be unacceptable, such as the world's most authoritarian and reclusive regime, North Korea. I'm very happy with the results of this project, as the measurements for Chongjin line up almost perfectly with UN Population estimates, and you can even see how the famine in the 1990s affected the development of Hamhung.

The Second Battle of Lexington

Documenting Racial Violence in Kentucky (Internship), Fall 2023

This internship tasked us with documenting instances of racial violence in Kentucky. While this was usually a case-by-case, person-to-person basis, in this project I got to track a larger incident that was a walk away from the UK campus. I got experience making a map based off an image, which I had to georefrence and trace (in this case, Sanborn Maps) as well as looking at multiple sources to see where certain things were (for example, the Tobacco Warehouse).
